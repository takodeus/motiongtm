GTM Beyond the Room
The activations behind the numbers
Cherre’s industry position extends well beyond conference floors. A sustained program of earned media, speaking invitations, industry awards, and thought leadership ensures the brand compounds between events.
The Full House
Serialized Brand Universe · Year-Long Campaign · 4 National Events · 2025
+
Most B2B companies treat trade shows as isolated moments — a booth, a happy hour, a follow-up email. Cherre built a year-long narrative arc across four national events, turning a conference calendar into a serialized collector experience. Each event dropped a limited-edition playing card. The first — the Ace of Spades at RETCON — launched through pure guerrilla scarcity: no booth, no signage, just a card you could only get by meeting the team face-to-face. That single mechanic created the demand that made every subsequent drop more desirable. By the fourth card at CREtech New York, attendees were seeking out the Cherre booth specifically to complete their set and earn the #StayCurious blind box mailer. The innovation: a loyalty and community mechanic borrowed from consumer brands — applied to enterprise real estate for the first time.
The Data Mart
A Data Ontology Universe · Immersive Experiential · OPTECH 2025 & RETCON 2026
+
Data ontology is Cherre’s core technical differentiator — and the hardest concept in its entire product suite to explain. Rather than default to slides and demos, Cherre made the problem physical. At OPTECH in Las Vegas, the booth became a grocery store where ontology breaks down: mislabeled products, misplaced aisles, SQL & Vinegar Chips shelved next to the produce. Attendees hunted ingredients for Curious Cupcakes and experienced firsthand what real estate data management feels like without a governing schema. At RETCON 2026, the universe extended with the OntoloTea speakeasy — a walk-in beverage cooler hidden inside the Cherre Mart, with koozies reading “Talk Data to Me,” “Prompt Responsibly,” and “Is This AI Generated.” The innovation: turning an abstract infrastructure concept into a tactile, shareable experience that generated more booth traffic and conversation than any product demo could.
The Studio
Street Vendor Speakeasy Kiosk Activation · October 2025
+
The final Full House card dropped alongside an activation designed for the city it was in: a NYC street vendor kiosk on the conference floor — checkered hotdog bags, a commissioned artist, 3D-printed merch. The activation ran simultaneously with the official launch of Agent STUDIO and the Cherre Agent Marketplace, converting booth energy directly into product awareness. Nearly twenty Cherre team members worked the floor — the largest single-company presence at CREtech that year. The innovation: using a collectible campaign’s final chapter to drive urgency to the booth at the exact moment Cherre had an important product story to tell.

The Position
Most vendors host a dinner for twenty. Cherre convenes two hundred and meets thirty of them one-on-one in the same week. Three years running.
That capability is not a marketing budget you can spend your way into. It is the consequence of three years of consistent presence, executive-level relationships, and trust earned at the senior leadership of every category that matters in institutional real assets: REITs, capital allocators, asset managers, developers, and the service ecosystem that surrounds them.
This position was not built through sponsorship budgets alone. It was built through creative activations that made Cherre the brand people talk about between conferences — serialized campaigns that turned passive attendees into a collector community, immersive experiences that made data ontology unforgettable, and a consistent commitment to giving back at every activation. The industry doesn’t just know Cherre. The industry looks forward to what Cherre does next.
For a strategic acquirer evaluating Cherre, the question is not whether this position has commercial value. It is whether they want to absorb the position or compete against the company that holds it.
